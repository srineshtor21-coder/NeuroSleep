Sleep Optimization Tips

☀️
Morning Light Exposure

Get 10 to 30 minutes of bright outdoor light within 30 minutes of waking. This anchors your circadian clock and promotes earlier, more consistent sleep onset each night.

📷
Screen Cutoff Timing

Eliminate blue light exposure at least 60 to 90 minutes before bed. Blue wavelength light (460 to 480 nm) suppresses melatonin by up to 50 percent and delays sleep onset.

🏧
Temperature Regulation

A cool bedroom (65 to 68 degrees F / 18 to 20 degrees C) facilitates the core body temperature drop required to initiate and maintain deep slow-wave sleep.

🕐
Consistent Wake Time

Maintaining a fixed wake time every day, including weekends, is the single most effective intervention for improving circadian alignment and sleep quality over time.
