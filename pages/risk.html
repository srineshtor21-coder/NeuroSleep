Model Weighting

Each factor is weighted based on its relative impact on glymphatic function and Alzheimer's risk per the Lancet 2020 Commission and subsequent meta-analyses.

35%
Sleep ConsistencyIrregular schedules disrupt BMAL1/CLOCK gene expression and reduce glymphatic clearance efficiency. SD above 45 min is associated with 27% greater cognitive risk in published studies.
30%
Sleep DurationBoth insufficient sleep (under 6h) and excessive sleep (over 9h) are linked to elevated amyloid-beta accumulation and accelerated cognitive decline trajectories.
20%
Screen ExposureBlue light (460-480nm) before bed suppresses melatonin by up to 50%, delays circadian phase, and reduces the slow-wave sleep depth needed for amyloid clearance.
15%
Cognitive PerformanceDeclining cognitive scores, especially reaction time variability, may reflect early neural changes that precede clinical Alzheimer's symptoms by years or decades.

IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

This calculator models behavioral risk factors only. It does not account for genetic risk (APOE e4 genotype), family history, or clinical biomarkers. A score in any range should not be interpreted as a medical diagnosis. Consult a healthcare provider for comprehensive Alzheimer's risk assessment.

Based on: Livingston et al. (2020). Dementia prevention, intervention, and care: 2020 report of the Lancet Commission. The Lancet, 396(10248), 413-446.
