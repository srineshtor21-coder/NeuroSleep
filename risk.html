📚 How The Score Is Calculated

The Alzheimer's Risk Behavior Score is a weighted composite model based on four modifiable behavioral domains, each linked to neurological risk in peer-reviewed literature:

35%
Sleep Consistency

Irregular schedules disrupt circadian biology and reduce glymphatic clearance. SD above 45 min is associated with 27% greater cognitive risk.

30%
Sleep Duration

Both insufficient sleep (under 6h) and excessive sleep (over 9h) are linked to elevated amyloid-beta accumulation and cognitive decline.

20%
Screen Exposure

Blue light exposure before bed suppresses melatonin, delays circadian phase, and reduces slow-wave sleep depth needed for amyloid clearance.

15%
Cognitive Performance

Declining cognitive scores, particularly reaction time variability, may reflect early neural changes that precede clinical symptoms of Alzheimer's disease.
